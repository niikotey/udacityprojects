Introduction¶

The medical database contains information about a 100000 patients and focuses on their medical appointments. There is information about the scheduled days for the appointments, the appointment days, their ages, neighbourhoods, health conditions, whether or not they received reminders for their appointments and also whether or not they showed up for their appointment.

Different factors or reasons could have caused the patients to miss their appointments. In this notebook, different factors would be explored as to why patients missed their appointments. Age, sex, whether a patient received a reminder or whether a patient is enrolled in the welfare program would be explored to see if it affects the ability of patients to make it for their appointments.

Some questions that could be answered through this project are as follows:¶

1. Does the gender of a patient determine whether they show up for an appointment?
2. Does the age of a patient determine whether they show up for an appointment?
3. Does a patient showing up for an appointment depend on receiving an SMS?
4. Does a patient having a scholarship determine whether they show up for an appointment?
5. Does a patient showing up for their appointment depend on how far their neighbourhood is from the hospital?
6. Does a patient showing up for their appointment depend on their health condition?

def plot(value1, value2, title, xlabel, ylabel):
    fig, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=(12,8))
    ax.hist(value1, alpha=0.7, label='No')
    ax.hist(value2, alpha=0.7, label='Yes')
    ax.set_title(title)
    ax.set_xlabel(xlabel)
    ax.set_ylabel(ylabel)
    ax.legend(loc='upper right')
    plt.show()

Conclusions¶

Findings¶

1. It was found that there are more females than there are males and most of both genders showed up for their appointments.
2. Patients between the ages of 40-60 years showed up for their appointments.
3. Patients who received an SMS showed up for their appointments, same as those who were enrolled in the Brazil welfare program.
4. Only a few patients (about 20-25%) were on the welfare program and still missed their appointments.

Limitations¶

1. There was no way to find out if reminders were sent to everyone or only those who did not show up and how soon they were sent.
1. The data could have been explored more using the health conditions of the patients.
2. An entire row was dropped due to the negative age of a patient which might have been keyed in wrongly.

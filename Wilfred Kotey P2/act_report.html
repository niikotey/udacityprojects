def plot(value1, value2, label, title):
    df.groupby(value1)[value2].mean().plot(kind='bar', rot=30)
    plt.ylabel(label);
    plt.title(title);

plot('stages', 'favorites', 'Average Like Count', 'Most liked Dog Stage')

People seem to like dogs in the puppo stage a lot more than the others.

A scatter plot can also be drawn to explore the relationship between retweet and like counts¶

In [6]:
# Scatter plot to show relationship between retweets and likes
df.plot(x = 'retweet', y = 'favorites', kind = 'scatter')
plt.xlabel('Retweet Count')
plt.ylabel('Favorites Count')
plt.title('Correlation between Retweets and Likes');

It can be observed that there is a positive correlation between retweets and likes, that is, any post that had a lot of likes had a lot of retweets as well. However, some tweets were retweeted a lot but had no likes.

Conclusion¶

Once the data was gathered, assessed and cleaned successful various insights could be made from the data. This includes knowing with growth stage of dogs people liked more per the retweets and likes on each tweet about the dog. This also showed a positive correlation for retweets and likes on each tweet as the more people liked a tweet about a dog the more they retweeted that tweet. Finally, it was also observed that there were more dogs in the pupper stage than any other dog stage in the dataset.
